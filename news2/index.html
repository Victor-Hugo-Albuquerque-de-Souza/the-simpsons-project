"My bagels are nothing but week-old doughnuts"
says grocery store owner

Customers are eating spoiled food since 1989

Springfield's new bombshell is about to explode. Our main grocery store, the Kuik-e-Mart has been selling poison food to the citizens for more than 30 years, according to Mr.X's investigation. The newsman has intercepted a conversation where the owner, Apu Nahasapeemapetilon confessed to a person that he usually doesn't dispose old doughnuts, but instead of it, he does some cleaning, change the products label and sell them as if they were fresh new ones. As stated by the rules of FTC (Federal Trade Comission), the expired products cannot be sold at all, and the store that trade them can be shut down for good, beside its owner can be put in prison. At ten o'clock yesterday, mr. Nahasapeemapetilon was arrested by the local police, and will be accused of selling expired food.

Police Chief Wigum interrupts press conference after embarassing questions

At Police Station, after the arrest, the Police Chief Clancy Wigum stated that '...in the interest of public safety...we confiscated every doughnut, bagel, cruller, and bear claw in the city'. After being asked about the rumours that the Police Station officers use the electric chair to cook chicken, Wigum called out the day and ended the press conference all of a sudden.
